**In South Africa the Land Cruiser 300 Series tyre size depends on the grade.** The GX-R and GR Sport run a stock **265/65R18** on 18-inch rims, while the ZX runs **265/55R20** on 20-inch rims.

## Stock and Upgrade Sizes by Grade

| 300 Series Grade | Stock Size | Common Upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| GX-R | 265/65R18 | 285/65R18 |
| GR Sport | 265/65R18 | 285/65R18 |
| ZX | 265/55R20 | 285/55R20 |

This is the model where assuming "one Land Cruiser tyre size" goes wrong. The off-road GX-R and GR Sport share the 18-inch wheel, while the luxury ZX flagship sits on 20s.

## Which Grade Upsizes Best

If tyres matter to your build, grade choice matters. The **18-inch GX-R and GR Sport** have the most to work with — 285/65R18 (around 33 inches) is the popular all-terrain upgrade and fits with little or no modification, giving extra sidewall for airing down off-road. It's no accident that Toyota put the off-road-focused **GR Sport on 18s rather than 20s**: the smaller rim leaves room for a taller, tougher tyre.

The **ZX on 20-inch rims** is the catch. Its 265/55R20 is a low-profile, road-biased tyre, and the big wheel leaves little sidewall and a narrower aftermarket choice. The 285/55R20 upgrade is about as far as it sensibly goes on the standard wheel — which is why serious off-road ZX owners often fit a smaller-diameter 18-inch wheel to open up the all-terrain range.

As with the 200, the 300 carries **TPMS** — keep the sensors and have them reset when you change tyres so you're not driving with a warning light.
