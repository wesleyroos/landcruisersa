**The Land Cruiser 300 Series tyre size depends on the trim.** The GX-R runs 265/65R18, the VX and ZX run 285/60R18, and the GR Sport runs 275/55R20. Find your trim in the table below for the stock size and the common upgrade.

## Stock and Upgrade Sizes by Trim

| 300 Series Trim | Stock Size | Common Upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| GX-R | 265/65R18 | 275/65R18 |
| VX / ZX | 285/60R18 | 285/65R18 |
| GR Sport | 275/55R20 | 285/55R20 |

This is the model where assuming "one Land Cruiser tyre size" goes wrong. The off-road-focused GX-R and the luxury VX/ZX both use 18-inch rims but different tyres, while the flagship GR Sport sits on 20-inch wheels with a low-profile tyre.

## Which Trim Upsizes Best

If tyres matter to your build, trim choice matters. The **18-inch GX-R and VX/ZX** have the most room to work with — 275/65R18 and 285/65R18 respectively get you toward a 33-inch tyre with extra sidewall for airing down, and they're the natural pick for an overland-leaning 300.

The **GR Sport on 20-inch rims** is the catch. Its 275/55R20 is a low-profile, performance-biased tyre, and the big wheel leaves little sidewall and a narrower aftermarket choice. The 285/55R20 upgrade is about as far as it sensibly goes on the standard wheel; serious off-road builds often fit a smaller-diameter 18-inch wheel to open up tyre options.

As with the 200, the 300 carries **TPMS** — keep the sensors and have them reset when you change tyres so you're not driving with a warning light.
