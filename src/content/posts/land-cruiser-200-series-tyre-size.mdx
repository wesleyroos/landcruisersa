**The Land Cruiser 200 Series runs a stock tyre size of 285/60R18.** The most common upgrade is **285/65R18** — roughly a 33-inch tyre — which adds sidewall height for off-road work while still fitting the standard setup.

## Stock and Upgrade Sizes

| 200 Series | Stock Size | Common Upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| All variants | 285/60R18 | 285/65R18 |

Unlike the older 16-inch Cruisers, the 200 runs 18-inch rims with a lower-profile tyre from the factory. The 285/65R18 upgrade keeps the same 18-inch wheel but trades some of that low profile for extra sidewall — better for absorbing rough tracks and dropping pressure in sand.

## What the 18-inch Rims and TPMS Mean

Two things shape tyre choice on the 200. First, the **18-inch rims**: combined with independent front suspension, they cap how far you can sensibly go. 285/65R18 (33s) is the practical sweet spot — bigger than that and you're into a lift plus arch and steering-clearance work, with diminishing returns on a heavy luxury wagon that spends most of its life on tar.

Second, the **tyre pressure monitoring system (TPMS)**. When you swap tyres or wheels, the sensors need to be retained and reset, or you'll live with a warning light on the dash. It's a quick job for a proper fitment centre — just flag it when you book.
