**In South Africa the Land Cruiser 200 Series tyre size depends on the grade.** The GX runs a stock **285/65R17** on 17-inch rims, while the VX and VX-R run **285/60R18** on 18-inch rims.

## Stock and Upgrade Sizes by Grade

| 200 Series Grade | Stock Size | Common Upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| GX | 285/65R17 | 285/70R17 |
| VX / VX-R | 285/60R18 | 285/65R18 |

Both stock sizes come out around 31–32 inches; the difference is rim diameter and sidewall. The 17-inch GX has the taller sidewall, the 18-inch VX/VX-R the lower-profile, more road-biased look.

## Which Grade Upsizes Best, and the TPMS Catch

Two things shape tyre choice on the 200. First, **grade and rim size**. The 17-inch GX is quietly the better off-road starting point: 285/70R17 (about 33 inches) gives generous sidewall for airing down in sand, and fits a stock or mildly lifted vehicle. The 18-inch VX/VX-R steps up to 285/65R18 for the equivalent diameter with less sidewall. Either way, combined with the 200's independent front suspension, going beyond roughly 33 inches means a lift plus arch and steering-clearance work, with diminishing returns on a heavy luxury wagon that lives mostly on tar.

Second, the **tyre pressure monitoring system (TPMS)**. When you swap tyres or wheels, the sensors need to be retained and reset, or you'll live with a warning light on the dash. It's a quick job for a proper fitment centre — just flag it when you book.
