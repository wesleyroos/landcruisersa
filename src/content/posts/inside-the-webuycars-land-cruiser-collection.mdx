There's a particular kind of quiet that settles over you when you walk into a room and realise you're standing in front of sixty years of Land Cruiser history, all in one place, all beautifully kept. We found that quiet this morning.

We'd heard there was a collection. We did not quite appreciate what that meant until we were standing in the middle of it.

## How we got in

Full disclosure: we didn't have an appointment. We rocked up at the WeBuyCars branch, said we'd come to see the Land Cruisers, and were politely asked whether we were expected. We weren't — but we mentioned we'd spoken to Wynand earlier in the week and that he'd said to come have a look.

The moment the name **Land Cruiser SA** landed, the temperature changed. Suddenly we were among friends. They called through to the branch's head mechanic — **Wade** — who, it turned out, was exactly the right person to show us around. Wade walked us through the entire collection, vehicle by vehicle, and his enthusiasm was infectious. He said how good it was to have actual Land Cruiser people come through — people who wanted to look properly, ask the right questions, and talk about the cars. We could've stayed all day. Wade, if you're reading this: thank you.

## Sixty years in one room

The collection belongs to **Faan van der Walt**, co-founder and CEO of WeBuyCars — and, very evidently, a serious Land Cruiser man. The number we kept hearing was around **50 vehicles**. There were roughly 30 on the floor the morning we visited, which brings us to the first lovely detail: this is not a static museum. It's a *living* collection. Faan, we're told, will come and grab one and drive it for a while; vehicles rotate in and out. The line-up you see today won't be exactly the line-up next month. These cars get used, which is rather the point of a Land Cruiser.

![A long line of Toyota Land Cruisers from across the decades inside the collection](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-70-anniversary-row.jpg)

What struck us most was how *complete* the spread is. This isn't a pile of one model. It's the whole family tree, generation by generation.

## The vintage core

It starts where the legend starts: the **FJ40**. There's a beautifully kept example up front, all round headlights and chrome TOYOTA grille — the shape that built the reputation.

Then the one that stopped us in our tracks: a gorgeous green **FJ45**, the long-wheelbase pickup. (For the trainspotters: the FJ43 is the medium-wheelbase 40-series, the FJ45 the long one — and a clean one like this is a genuine rarity.)

![A restored green Toyota FJ45 long-wheelbase Land Cruiser](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-green-fj45.jpg)

From there into the **60-Series** — including the white FJ62 and, best of all, an '80s wagon wearing the original red-and-yellow "Four Wheel Drive" side stripe. If you grew up in the back of one of these, that stripe just sent you straight back.

![The iconic red and yellow side stripe on an 1980s Toyota Land Cruiser 60-Series](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-60-series-stripe.jpg)

The bloodline continues unbroken from there: an **80-Series**, a **100-Series GXL** (the 4.2 turbo-diesel — the smooth, independent-front-suspension touring one, as opposed to the solid-axle 105), and the luxury **200** and **300-Series** wagons holding down the modern end.

## The modern 70-Series — and the last of a line

Faan has gone deep on the current 70-Series, which is the smart move: these are the **last of an era**. There's the new-shape **76 wagon**, the **79 single cab** and **double cab**, and — the collector's pieces — the **70th Anniversary editions**, with their heritage grilles, retro "Toyota Land Cruiser" script badges and commemorative trim, marking 70 years of the nameplate. There's even a safari-spec open game-viewer in the mix, and an **FJ Cruiser** for good measure.

## Wynand's 79

One of the cars isn't Faan's at all — it belongs to **Wynand Beukes**, WeBuyCars' deputy CEO. It's a 2007 **79** that started life as a single cab and has since been **converted to a double cab**, and the conversion work is genuinely impressive — Wade walked us through it, and while you can spot it if you know where to look, it's been done properly.

![Wynand's beige Toyota Land Cruiser 79 double cab, a 2007 single-cab conversion](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-wynand-79.jpg)

It's a fitting car for a Land Cruiser man to keep among this company — a working bakkie, personalised, and clearly loved.

## The mystery in black

And then there's the one we're still chasing down. A black short-wheelbase 70-Series wagon, white roof, sitting quietly in the corner wearing a gold **"Land Cruiser 50 Years"** badge. It's the kind of car that comes with a reputation attached — and we'd rather get its full story straight than repeat a legend, so we'll come back to this one properly. If you know exactly what it is, we'd love to hear from you.

![A rare black short-wheelbase Toyota Land Cruiser 70-Series wagon with a Land Cruiser 50 Years badge](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-black-50-years.jpg)

## This is a first look — we'll be back

We walked in on a whim and walked out an hour later slightly star-struck. This piece is exactly what it says it is: a first look. The collection deserves a proper sit-down — the full story of how Faan came to build it, the history behind the standout cars, and photographs that do them justice. That's the article we really want to write, and we're hoping to.

For now, our thanks to Wade and the WeBuyCars team for the unplanned tour and the warm welcome. It's a special thing to see this many Land Cruisers, this well cared for, under one roof — and to be reminded why, in this country, this badge means what it does.
