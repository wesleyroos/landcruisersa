There's a particular kind of quiet that settles over you when you walk into a room and find sixty years of Land Cruiser history lined up in front of you. That's the feeling of standing inside **Faan van der Walt's Land Cruiser collection** — the personal collection of the **WeBuyCars founder**, around 50 Cruisers kept quietly at a branch in Pretoria, and very possibly the most complete private Land Cruiser collection in South Africa.

We'd heard there was a collection. We did not quite appreciate what that meant until we were standing in the middle of it.

## A warm welcome

When we arrived at the WeBuyCars Silverlakes branch in Pretoria and explained that we were from Land Cruiser SA, the welcome was immediate and genuine — these are clearly people who love these cars as much as we do. We were introduced to the branch's head mechanic, **Wade**, who turned out to be exactly the right person to show us around.

Wade walked us through the entire collection, vehicle by vehicle, and his enthusiasm was infectious. He spoke about how good it is to have fellow Land Cruiser people come through — people who want to look properly, ask the right questions, and talk about the cars. We could have stayed all day. Wade, if you're reading this: thank you.

## Sixty years of Land Cruisers in one room

The collection belongs to **Faan van der Walt**, the co-founder and CEO of WeBuyCars — the man who, with his brother Dirk, turned a boyhood spent fixing cars in Bronkhorstspruit into South Africa's biggest used-car business. He is, very evidently, also a serious Land Cruiser man. The number we kept hearing was around **50 vehicles**. There were roughly 30 on the floor the morning we visited, which brings us to the first lovely detail: this is not a static museum. It's a *living* collection. Faan, we're told, will come and grab one and drive it for a while; vehicles rotate in and out. The line-up you see today won't be exactly the line-up next month. These cars get used, which is rather the point of a Land Cruiser. And the collection isn't only Faan's: it's reported that **Wynand Beukes**, WeBuyCars' deputy CEO, also owns one of the vehicles among them.

![A long line of Toyota Land Cruisers from across the decades inside the collection](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-70-anniversary-row-c.jpg)

What struck us most was how *complete* the spread is. This isn't a pile of one model. It's the whole family tree, generation by generation.

## The vintage core: FJ40, FJ45 and 60-Series

It starts where the legend starts: the **FJ40**. There's a beautifully kept example up front, all round headlights and chrome TOYOTA grille — the shape that built the reputation.

![A beige Toyota FJ40 Land Cruiser in Faan van der Walt's collection](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-fj40-c.jpg)

Then the one that stopped us in our tracks: a gorgeous green **FJ45**, the long-wheelbase pickup. (For the trainspotters: the FJ43 is the medium-wheelbase 40-series, the FJ45 the long one — and a clean one like this is a genuine rarity.)

![A restored green Toyota FJ45 long-wheelbase Land Cruiser](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-green-fj45-c.jpg)

From there into the **60-Series** — including the white FJ62 and, best of all, an '80s wagon wearing the original red-and-yellow "Four Wheel Drive" side stripe. If you grew up in the back of one of these, that stripe just sent you straight back.

![The iconic red and yellow side stripe on an 1980s Toyota Land Cruiser 60-Series](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-60-series-stripe-c.jpg)

The bloodline continues unbroken from there: an **80-Series**, a [**100-Series GXL**](/market/100-series/) (the 4.2 turbo-diesel — the smooth, independent-front-suspension touring one, as opposed to the solid-axle 105), and the luxury **200** and [**300-Series**](/market/300-series/) wagons holding down the modern end.

## The 70th Anniversary 70-Series

Where Faan has really gone deep is the **70th Anniversary** editions — and it's a smart place to focus, because these are the genuine collector's pieces. Released to mark 70 years of the Land Cruiser, they wear unique anniversary badging, a heritage-style grille with retro "Toyota Land Cruiser" lettering, and commemorative trim. He has them across the range: the [**76 wagon**](/market/76-series/), and the [**79**](/market/79-series/) in both **single cab** and **double cab**.

![A Toyota Land Cruiser 70th Anniversary edition, showing the commemorative badge and the retro "Toyota Land Cruiser" heritage script](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-70th-anniversary.jpg)

Alongside the anniversary cars sit a safari-spec open game-viewer and an **FJ Cruiser**, for good measure.

## A clever conversion

One car in particular caught our eye: a **79** that started life as a single cab and has since been **converted into a double cab**. The work is genuinely impressive — Wade walked us through it, and while you can spot the conversion if you know exactly where to look, it's been done properly, the kind of job that takes real skill and patience.

![A beige Toyota Land Cruiser 79 double cab that began life as a single cab](https://pub-6c900fb2e73a4b89bc049099101e4591.r2.dev/images/posts/faan-collection/faan-collection-79-conversion.jpg)

It's exactly the sort of personalised, well-loved working bakkie that makes a collection like this feel alive rather than static.

## The black HZJ73 — a 50th Anniversary edition

The car we kept coming back to: a black **HZJ73** with a white removable roof, wearing a gold **"Land Cruiser 50 Years"** badge.

The HZJ73 is the three-door 70-Series wagon — powered by the legendary **1HZ 4.2-litre naturally-aspirated diesel** and a five-speed manual, and topped with the removable **FRP (fibreglass) hardtop** that defined these wagons. They were never an everyday sight on South African roads; this is a go-anywhere 70-Series with a near-mythical reputation among Cruiser people.

The gold **"50 Years"** badge tells the rest of the story: this is a **50th Anniversary edition**, built to mark fifty years of the Land Cruiser (1951–2001). Rumour has it only a handful ever made it to South Africa — and a clean, anniversary-badged HZJ73 like this is about as special as a 70-Series gets.

## This is a first look — we'll be back

We came for a quick look and walked out an hour later slightly star-struck. This piece is exactly what it says it is: a first look. The collection deserves a proper sit-down — the full story of how Faan came to build it, the history behind the standout cars, and photographs that do them justice. That's the article we really want to write, and we're hoping to.

For now, our thanks to Wade and the WeBuyCars team for the tour and the warm welcome. It's a special thing to see this many Land Cruisers, this well cared for, under one roof — and to be reminded why, in this country, this badge means what it does.

*If you'd like to see the collection for yourself, it lives at the **WeBuyCars Silverlakes branch** in Pretoria. It's worth arranging a viewing ahead of time if you want a proper look around — otherwise you may only catch a glimpse of it from the showroom floor.*
