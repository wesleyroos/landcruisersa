A game-viewer conversion turns a Land Cruiser — usually a [79 Series](/listings/model/79-series/) single cab pick-up — into an open-sided, 9 to 11-seat safari vehicle with raised, tiered bench seating. The **conversion work itself costs about R95,000 to R175,000**, on top of the donor vehicle. That second half of the sentence is the part most buyers miss, and it decides everything: whether converting is a bargain or a mistake depends almost entirely on the donor. This guide covers the real numbers, what the work involves, which base vehicle to use, and who actually builds these conversions in South Africa.

## Donor cost vs conversion cost — the number nobody explains

When you see a new turnkey Land Cruiser 79 game viewer listed at **R1.3 million or more**, the conversion is not what you're paying for. You're paying for a **brand-new Land Cruiser 79 with a conversion on top**. Split the two and the maths gets much more interesting:

| Component | Cost |
|---|---|
| **Conversion — standard vehicle** (roof cut, 3 bench rows, canvas upholstery, paint) | ~R95,000 |
| **Conversion — truck** (5 bench rows, 10 safety doors and steps) | ~R175,000 |
| **Donor vehicle** (used LC79, depending on age/mileage) | the rest — typically several hundred thousand rand |

So the route to a game viewer at the lowest cost is obvious: **supply the donor yourself.** If you already own a suitable Cruiser, you're R95,000–R175,000 away from a game viewer. If you don't, source a good used one first — [browse the current Land Cruiser 79 listings](/listings/model/79-series/) and check the [79 Series market data](/market/79-series/) to see what donors are actually asking. A clean, higher-mileage 79 plus a R95k conversion can land well under half the price of a turnkey build.

The comparison to keep in mind: used, already-converted game viewers currently list from roughly **R190,000 to R1.6 million** (median around R750,000) — the full breakdown is in our [game viewer prices & buyer's guide](/useful-info/game-viewer-price-buyers-guide-south-africa/), and the live spread is on the [Game Viewers page](/game-viewers/).

## What a conversion actually involves

A professional game-viewer conversion is major body surgery, not an accessory fit. A typical build includes:

- **Roof cut and removal** — the cab and load area are opened up to the open-sided safari configuration;
- **Tiered bench seating** — usually three raised rows on a standard vehicle, up to five on a truck conversion, stepped so every row sees over the one in front;
- **Safety doors and steps** — larger truck conversions carry up to ten safety doors plus access steps;
- **Canvas upholstery** — seating trimmed for sun, dust and rain;
- **Rubberising and paint** — the load floor and body protected for bush work, finished in the operator's colours;
- extras by builder and spec — cooler/rack mounting, canopies, guide seats.

One consequence matters more than the rest: **changing the body and the seat count is a legal modification.** Under South African law, altering the number of passenger seats pulls the vehicle into the national compulsory-specification regime administered by the NRCS, and the vehicle must be registered and pass roadworthy in its converted form. If you'll carry paying passengers, there's a second layer — an NPTR operating licence, a PrDP-holding driver and public passenger liability insurance. The full breakdown (including the 2009 first-registration cut-off that catches buyers of older vehicles) is in the [road-legal section of the buyer's guide](/useful-info/game-viewer-price-buyers-guide-south-africa/). A reputable converter will know this terrain well — ask how the paperwork is handled before you commit; and as always, confirm current requirements with the NRCS/NPTR, as this is general guidance, not legal advice.

## Which base vehicle?

- **Land Cruiser 79 single cab** — the default for commercial fleets, and what most converters build on. The ladder-frame chassis and solid axles handle years of slow, heavy, low-range game-drive work; parts are available everywhere from Hoedspruit to Maun; and no safari vehicle holds its value better.
- **[78 Series Troopcarrier](/listings/model/78-series/)** — the enclosed wagon alternative, converted where a different body style suits the operation.
- **FJ and Prado** — used for lighter-duty and private conversions rather than lodge fleets.
- Factory alternative: if the budget is the deciding factor, Mahindra sells a factory-built game viewer new from about R521,599 — the [Land Cruiser vs Mahindra comparison](/useful-info/game-viewer-price-buyers-guide-south-africa/) covers that fork.

These are established South African specialists (listed for reference, not as endorsements — get more than one quote):

- **[Safari Game Viewers](https://safarigameviewers.co.za)** (Pretoria) — manufactures safari vehicles for private game farms and lodges, converts Land Cruiser FJ and Prado bases, and exports to Botswana, Zambia and Namibia.
- **[Maruleng Auto](https://marulengauto.co.za)** (Hoedspruit, Limpopo) — right on Kruger's doorstep; builds, customises, sells and services game viewers as a one-stop operation, including SANParks-compliant vehicles.
- **[Meano & Sons](https://meanoconversions.co.za)** — builds most of its safari-operator conversions on the Toyota Land Cruiser pick-up, including full conversions into a five-door station wagon.
- **[Baillies Off Road](https://baillies.co.za)** — a Land Cruiser specialist with over 30 years' experience across conversions, traybacks and accessories.
- **[SafariQuip](https://safariquip.co.za)** (Pretoria) — builds game viewers on Land Cruiser 76 and 79, Mahindra mHawk S4 and Suzuki Jimny bases — the widest range of donor platforms among the specialists here — and also handles refurbishment, upgrades and off-road camper trailers, with builds exported into the region.

## Convert, or buy one already done?

**Convert your own when:**
- you already own a suitable Cruiser, or can source a well-priced used donor;
- you want to choose the exact configuration (rows, doors, colours, extras);
- you can afford the build time before the vehicle earns its keep.

**Buy ready-made when:**
- you need the vehicle working this season — [see every game viewer currently for sale](/game-viewers/);
- you want the conversion already on the licence and through roadworthy;
- a lodge fleet retirement puts a well-maintained example in front of you at the right price.

## The bottom line

The conversion is the cheap part: **R95,000–R175,000** buys the roof cut, tiered seating, safety doors and trim that turn a Land Cruiser into a game viewer.
