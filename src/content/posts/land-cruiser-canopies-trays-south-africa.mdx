Walk any overlanding campsite from the Kgalagadi to Kosi Bay and you'll see the same truth: almost nobody runs a Land Cruiser bakkie with a bare load bin. The back of a Cruiser is a platform, and South Africa has built an entire industry on what goes onto it — from a farmer's plain aluminium canopy to half-million-rand expedition bodies that sleep a family of four.

This guide maps the whole ladder: what each setup is, who builds it in SA, what it genuinely costs, and which one fits how you actually use your Cruiser.

> **Quick answer:** There are five tiers, not two. **(1) A canopy** on the standard load bin — aluminium from ~R34k entry level, premium from Bushtech/RSI higher. **(2) A tray** (flat deck) replacing the bin — AluBlack's from R82,225. **(3) Tray + tray-canopy systems** — modular, slide-off setups from ~R152k. **(4) Canopy campers** — Alu-Cab's ModCAP/Canopy Camper turn the bin into a two-sleeper. **(5) Full-body conversions** — Bushwakka's Kamelback replaces the rear entirely, ±R547k excluding the vehicle. Aluminium beats fibreglass for anything serious, and 4x4 Mega World's 25+ branches are the biggest place to buy and fit.

## The ladder at a glance

| Tier | What it is | Realistic cost | Who it's for |

## Tier 1: The canopy — where almost everyone starts

The classic. A canopy encloses the load bin, keeps dust off your gear, locks, and carries a [rooftop tent](/useful-info/best-rooftop-tent-for-a-land-cruiser/) if it's built right. The material question settles most of the decision:

- **Aluminium — the SA default.** Strong, light and modular. **[Bushtech](https://bushtech.co.za/)** is the community benchmark: the 79 double-cab canopy weighs about **65kg**, owners on the 4x4 forums call it "103% dust-proof", and it comes with a **10-year frame warranty plus a free annual service for 10 years** — no one else in the market offers that. Bushtech sells through dealers like [Sheer Gear](https://sheergear.co.za/shop/land-cruiser-79-series-double-cab-bushtech-original-aluminium-canopy/) and HJM Offroad. On a budget, **[Big Country](https://bigcountry.co.za/shop/canopies/canopy-land-cruiser-79/)** lists a 79 canopy at **R33,599–R35,499** — the realistic entry price for new aluminium — with **Gomad** and **Zambezi** in the same value tier.
- **Stainless steel — the tank.** **[RSI SmartCap](https://www.sa.rsismartcap.com/lc-landcruiser)**'s EVO canopies are built from 409 automotive-grade stainless. Owner verdict from the forums: *"massively expensive and heavy… but a quality product."* If the Cruiser works a mine or a farm hard for a decade, this is the buy-once option.
- **Fibreglass — think twice on a Cruiser.** Smooth looks and lower prices, but the average fibreglass canopy weighs ~85kg (heavier than premium aluminium), cracks under point loads, and fibreglass units failing under rooftop tents is a recurring story. Fine for a mall bakkie; wrong for a working 79.

## Tier 2: The tray — the working answer

A tray (flat deck or dropside) removes the load bin entirely and replaces it with a flat, usually aluminium bed. You gain a bigger usable footprint (no wheel-arch intrusions), three-sided access, and honest tie-down points. This is standard practice on Australian 79s and it's growing fast here.

- **[AluBlack](https://alublack.com/)** (Buffeljagsrivier, founded 2022) builds trays for any single, extended or double cab — **from R82,225**.
- **[RMA Special Vehicles](https://sa.rmaspecialvehicles.com/blog/drop-side-conversions-for-light-commercial-vehicles)** does OEM-grade dropside conversions engineered to retain the vehicle's rated carrying capacity — the fleet and farming end.
- **WR Off Road** has developed a modular bin system specifically for the 79.

## Tier 3: Tray + canopy systems — the double life

Put a purpose-built canopy on that tray and you get the most flexible setup on this ladder. Two SA systems stand out:

- **[AluBlack's modular system](https://alublack.com/)** is the clever one: the canopy/camper module **slides off the tray completely** — work bakkie Monday, camper Friday, and the module can even travel on their matching off-road trailer. Tray + canopy starts around **R151,800** depending on extras (kitchen unit, 90L fridge slide, 270° awning, rooftop tent, solar — many of them Land Cruiser-specific), and they offer finance terms up to 60 months. International outlets have picked the system up as one of the most flexible truck-camping rigs anywhere.
- **[Bushtech's tray canopies](https://bushtech.co.za/tray-canopies/)** integrate the touring plumbing into the canopy itself: a built-in **75-litre water tank**, a rear hatch that doubles as a camp table, a trundle drawer with stainless top, and a **400kg dynamic roof load** rating — with the same 10-year warranty and service plan as their bin canopies.

## Tier 4: Canopy campers — the bin becomes a bedroom

A canopy camper looks like a canopy but lives like a camper: wedge pop-top roof, sleeping deck for two, and internal access from the cab. **[Alu-Cab](https://www.alu-cab.com/products/alu-cab-land-cruiser-modcap-camper/)** (Cape Town — the SA brand the whole world imports) owns this tier for the Land Cruiser:

- The **Canopy Camper** (Basic and Deluxe) is the established option for the 79.
- The **ModCAP** is the smart-money buy: it starts life as a flat-roof canopy for work and gear, then **upgrades into a full two-sleeper camper** when the budget or the trip demands it — you don't have to choose on day one.
- For Troopy owners (75/78), the **[Hercules roof conversion](https://www.alu-cab.com/products/alu-cab-hercules-roof-conversion/)** is the same idea applied to the roof: standing room inside and an elevated bed for two.

Prices are on request and spec-dependent; buy through **[4x4 Mega World](https://www.4x4megaworldonline.com/aluminium-canopies-accessories/)** — the official Alu-Cab distributor, with 25+ branches and a fitment centre at each — or dealers like HJM Offroad and All Terrain Africa.

## Tier 5: Full-body conversions — the rear is replaced entirely

At the top of the ladder the load bin is gone and a purpose-built habitat mounts directly to the chassis:

- **[Bushwakka's Kamelback](https://bushwakka.africa/kamelback-camper-conversion/)** (Swellendam district) is the reference build, and it's earned international coverage from New Atlas, Carscoops and Gear Patrol. Marine-grade aluminium body, **sleeping for four** (pop-top bed plus fold-out lower bed), full kitchen, 90L water with hot shower cubicle, lithium power with optional 380W solar — at roughly **R547,000 before you supply the Land Cruiser**.
- **Bushlore's CruCAM** is the same concept engineered for their rental fleet — you can [rent one](/useful-info/land-cruiser-rental-south-africa/) before you ever commit to building one, which is honestly the smartest way to test this tier.
- Custom shops — **Bruiser Cruiser**, **Baillies Off Road** (who also build [game-viewer bodies](/game-viewers/)) — quote to spec.

## The boring section that saves you money: weight, payload & paperwork

- **Payload maths first.** Everything on this ladder comes off your legal carrying capacity. A 65kg canopy is trivial; a tray + canopy + water + fridge + drawers stack isn't. Weigh the loaded vehicle against its GVM before the Kalahari, not after.
- **Dynamic roof ratings matter more than static.** Corrugations multiply loads. If a rooftop tent is going on top, check the *dynamic* rating (Bushtech's 400kg tray-canopy rating is the kind of number you want to see in writing).
- **Conversions change the vehicle.** A tray or full-body build alters the body configuration: reputable builders engineer to retain rated capacity (RMA states this explicitly) — get it in writing, keep the certificate, and tell your insurer.
- **Fitment is half the product.** Dust-proofing lives or dies on the seal and the fitment. Buy from someone who fits professionally — it's most of what you're paying for at the premium end.

## The verdict, by how you actually use your Cruiser

- **Farm or fleet workhorse:** a tray (RMA/AluBlack) or an entry aluminium canopy. Skip fibreglass.
- **Weekend tourer:** a premium aluminium canopy — Bushtech's warranty and dust-proofing are the community pick — with a [rooftop tent](/useful-info/best-rooftop-tent-for-a-land-cruiser/) on top.
- **Work-week, tour-weekend double life:** AluBlack's slide-off system or a Bushtech tray canopy. One vehicle, two jobs.
- **Serious overlander:** Alu-Cab ModCAP — start as a canopy, upgrade to the camper when ready.
- **Expedition or family touring:** the full-body tier — and rent a Bushlore CruCAM for a trip first; it's the cheapest R500k decision-insurance you'll ever buy.

Whatever tier you're on, the second-hand market matters too: canopies and conversions hold value well in SA, and kitted Cruisers wear them proudly — [browse the 79s currently for sale](/listings/model/79-series/) and count how many carry a canopy. That's not an accident; it's the most useful accessory this vehicle takes.
