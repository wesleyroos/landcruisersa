## What the whole picture says (the patterns)

1. **Build-rich, demand-poor — and the gap is widening.** Enormous sophisticated
   machinery (spec-aware valuation engine, finance capture, dealer loop, GSC integration,
   prediction ledger, IG flywheel, a *cloned* business in Jimny, a theft-content cluster)
   vs ~40 visitors/day, ~0 conversions, 0 paying customers, 1 captured sold price. The
   [[project-business-brain-strategy]] memo already diagnosed "traffic is the binding
   constraint," yet most asks since keep *adding surfaces*. WBC confirmed it from outside:
   they valued the brand/positioning, not the engine.

2. **Every piece of real momentum came from relationships, not the funnel.** Jimny
   (Tersia), the WBC meeting (Tersia), Book of Life (father-in-law/mSure), the dealer loop
   (personal contact). **Not one major opportunity originated from the AI-search/SEO
   flywheel.** The flywheel builds the *demo*; the demo opens *doors*; the doors hold the value.

3. **The ownable, exit-grade assets are the BRAND + AI-citability + the playbook — less so the raw data.**
   ⚠️ *Corrected later 2026-06-23:* WBC also scrapes the whole market and M&M-catalogs it, so the
   "unique cross-platform data moat" (positioning.md) holds vs single-portal AutoTrader but **NOT vs a
   data-rich aggregator like WBC.** What WBC can't trivially reproduce: the consumer-facing **niche brand
   + trust**, **AI-search citability** (the cited answer; their catalog is backend), and the **repeatable
   niche-vertical playbook** (Jimny). The 434 visitors who come stay 5+ min (high intent); the problem is
   volume — which B2B2C distribution sidesteps. (positioning.md's "nobody can replicate" claim needs this caveat.)

4. **Many loops opened, none closed.** Finance (no partner), dealer (deal #1 in flight,
   never closed), Ghost (monitoring), rental (authority-only by our own rule), IG (experiment).
   ~8 parallel revenue threads at 40 visitors/day, R0 produced. The next unit of effort is
   worth more *finishing* a loop than starting one.

## Standout data

- **AI citation is real & compounding** — ChatGPT 9 / Gemini 1 referrals in ~12 days from
  zero. Core thesis has early validation; small but trending right.
- **Asking-vs-sold is the biggest data hole** — 1 captured sold comp (79 single-cab, −27%
  vs ask). Engine capped at ~68% in-band without sold/spec data. **WBC has exactly this.**
- **Best-converting query (rental, #3.5, ~14% CTR) is the one we've chosen not to monetise**
  (no clean affiliate, per philosophy). The market pulls hardest where our rules forbid selling.

## The reframe (pivot-or-not, largely answered by the data)

**LCSA is not a consumer-traffic business with data. It is a data-and-relationships
business with a consumer site.** The site's real jobs: (1) generate the proprietary
dataset, (2) be the credibility/demo artifact that opens B2B doors, (3) prove the playbook
repeats (why Jimny exists). It is *not*, at this traffic, a consumer funnel to optimise as
if conversions are imminent.

**Priority inversion that follows:** B2B data/distribution deals (WBC, mSure) = A-priority.
Consumer content/SEO = maintenance that keeps the demo sharp + the dataset growing.
New consumer surfaces = C unless they directly feed a B2B story.

The WBC 8M-pageviews + AutoTrader-killer facts harden this: our value to them is the
**niche-vertical playbook + cross-platform data layer** AutoTrader structurally can't build.
That's an **acquisition thesis**, and WBC is the most plausible acquirer the exit mandate wants.

## Tightening the feedback loop (what changed today)

1. **Deal Ledger created** (`deal-ledger.md`) — instruments the channel that actually moves
   (B2B), with stage / next action / value hypothesis / kill-or-advance date per deal.
2. **Traction redefined** — at 40 visitors/day, pageviews can't be the scoreboard. The two
   metrics that matter: **(a) first rand from any loop, (b) signed B2B agreements.** (Both
   live at the top of `deal-ledger.md`.)
3. **WIP limit** — no new revenue surface until an existing loop produces a rand or is killed.
4. **One question per cycle** — this cycle: *"Does WBC convert to a signed API + sales-
   attribution integration?"* Yes → we've found the wedge + likely acquirer. Stall → data on
   whether the B2B-relationship channel is reliable or just flattering.
