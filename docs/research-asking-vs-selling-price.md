# Research: Asking vs Selling Price for Used Vehicles (SA + global)

*Deep multi-source research with adversarial fact-checking. 13 June 2026. 24 sources fetched, 72 claims extracted, 25 verified, 20 confirmed / 5 refuted.*

## Headline verdict

A systematic gap between asking and final transaction prices is **real and well-established in economics**, but **no credible source gives a clean "average % off asking price"** — not globally, and especially **not for South Africa or for 4x4/SUV/bakkie segments**. The honest article is NOT "cars sell for X% below asking." It's "here's what asking prices actually tell you, what the real reference points are, and why nobody publishes the gap you'd most want — which is exactly the gap our data is starting to fill."

## Confirmed, citable (use these)

1. **The gap is real and structurally recoverable.** Huang (2020), *J. Economics & Management Strategy* — used-car list prices carry unobserved discounts under haggling; whether a dealer holds firm depends on market power. US-specific; no headline %. [SSRN 1805298]
2. **Dealers out-negotiate private sellers.** NBER w34136 (2025, Ohio data) — dealers extract 20–50% more surplus per trade via selection, matching, and bargaining power. US, not 4x4-specific.
3. **Real bargaining leaves money on the table.** Larsen (2021), *Review of Economic Studies* — 17–24% of negotiating pairs fail to trade despite gains existing. **Scope caveat: wholesale dealer-to-dealer auctions, not retail** — use as theory, not a retail discount figure.
4. **TransUnion SA Vehicle Pricing Index** — used-vehicle price inflation −2.8% YoY, new +1.7% (Q4 2024). **Measures price-level change over time, NOT the negotiation gap.** Best citable SA price data but answers a different question.
5. **Trade vs Retail spread (~10–20%)** — TransUnion & multiple SA sources: the value a seller gets (trade) sits 10–20% below what a buyer pays (retail). The closest SA proxy for an asking-vs-real gap, but it's the dealer-buy/dealer-sell spread, not private asking-vs-final.
6. **SA used demand rising** — pre-owned financing outpaced new 1.56:1 (Q4 2024, TransUnion). Context, not gap data.
7. **Valuation authorities exist** — TransUnion (60k vehicles/mo, "realistic retail" ML model) and Lightstone Auto (~30 yrs, 2.8M+ bank transactions). Cite as "X states" — vendor marketing, not audited.
8. **The industry tracks transaction prices, not asking** — Edmunds Q3 2024: US used transaction price $27,177 (−6.2% YoY), 36 days-to-turn.

## REFUTED — do NOT cite (these are the tempting-but-wrong claims)

- ❌ "iSeeCars: dealers cut price 1–6× over 31.5 days, first drop ~5%" (0–3)
- ❌ "Wait 30 days for 5–15% off listing" (1–2)
- ❌ "US chains broadly adopted no-haggle pricing" (0–3)
- ❌ "2.31 used financed per new in SA Q4 2021" (1–2)

## The honest gaps (state these in the article — they're the differentiator)

- No public SA source links **listing price → final selling price** at retail.
- **Zero** evidence specific to 4x4/SUV/bakkie segments or by age/price band.
- Days-on-market correlates with price cuts but has a confounder: long DOM can mean overpriced/undesirable, not "about to drop." No clean quantified version survived fact-checking.

## Why this is good news for LCSA

The thing every buyer wants — "what's the real gap between asking and what these actually sell for" — **does not exist as a public SA number.** We are uniquely positioned to become that reference: we already record every asking-price change across every platform (`price_events`), and as listings delist we can observe last-asking-before-removal. With an honest "this is last asking, not confirmed sale price" label, that's a dataset nobody else in SA publishes — and the article is what plants the flag.
