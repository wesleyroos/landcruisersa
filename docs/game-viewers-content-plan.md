### Regulations (all 3-0 adversarially confirmed unless noted)

Two separate legal questions — keep them separate in the article:

**(A) Is the VEHICLE legal? (registration / roadworthy)**
- Homologation = the **NRCS**'s mandatory process proving a vehicle/model conforms to SA compulsory specifications. (nrcs.org.za) ✓
- **Modifications** — not just new models — must conform to compulsory specs. This is the hook that pulls a conversion into scope. (nrcs.org.za) ✓
- The legal definition of **"modify" explicitly includes "altering the number of passenger seats on a motor vehicle"** — precisely what a game-viewer conversion does. (NRCS MIB doc, NRTA reg 38) ✓
- **No vehicle may be sold, registered or licensed without a NATIS number** allocated by the NRCS. (nrcs.org.za) ✓
- A **Letter of Authority (LoA)** is mandatory before registering newly built or **modified** vehicles in SA (not only imports), **issued by the NRCS**. (gov.za) ✓
- **NRTA regulation 38:** any business that builds or modifies vehicles *for the purpose of selling them* must register as a **Manufacturer, Importer or Builder (MIB)**. Covers commercial conversion companies. (NRCS MIB doc) ✓

**(B) Is the OPERATION legal? (commercial game drives)**
- The **National Land Transport Act 2009** establishes the **National Public Transport Regulator (NPTR)**, which issues operating licences for tourist transport and accredits tour operators. (transport.gov.za NPTR Handbook) ✓
- Anyone providing tourist transport services **must hold an operating licence** (NLTA s50); the **only** exemption is courtesy services with **fewer than three cars**. (NPTR Handbook) ✓
- Operating-licence application requires: vehicle licence disc, **roadworthy certificate**, certificate of registration, service records/maintenance plan, and **Public Passenger Liability Insurance**. Vehicles for a *new* operating licence **must have been first registered from 2009 onwards.** (NPTR Handbook) ✓ — **hard age cut-off; critical for buyers of older used game viewers intending commercial use.**
- Drivers of accredited tourist transport **must hold a PrDP** (professional driving permit); accreditation can be cancelled for repeatedly using non-PrDP drivers. (NPTR Handbook) ✓
- A **PrDP is required** for drivers carrying passengers for reward / any vehicle requiring an operating licence. (gov.za) ✓
- No road-based public transport service — explicitly including **"tourism-based transport services"** — may operate without an operating licence issued **per vehicle**. (tourism.gov.za NLTA compendium) ✓

### ⚠️ REFUTED — do NOT publish these framings

Two claims failed verification (1-2). They point at a genuinely murky area — treat as **open questions needing professional verification**, not facts:
1. *"The Letter of Authority falls under the National Road Traffic Act 1996 and requires SABS conformity proof."* — the NRTA-1996 + SABS-specific framing was contested. The LoA is via NRCS under **compulsory specifications**; don't cite the specific Act number or SABS without checking.
2. *"Only a registered MIB can apply for homologation, so a converted game viewer must trace back to a registered builder to be registrable."* — over-reach. The verifiers disputed that a **privately** converted vehicle (owner converts own car, not for sale) can't be registered. **The private-owner registration path is the single biggest unknown — get it professionally verified.**

### 📄 Article 2 — "Do You Need a Licence to Operate a Game Viewer in South Africa?" ⭐ PRIORITY 2

- **Slug:** `/useful-info/game-viewer-licence-south-africa/`
- **Primary keyword:** game viewer licence / operating licence game drive south africa
- **Secondary:** roadworthy game viewer, homologation vehicle conversion SA, PrDP game drive, register a converted vehicle
- **Intent:** lodge/operator + private buyer checking legality — **pure whitespace; no competitor covers this.**
- **Why second (arguably the most valuable):** highest differentiation, fully verified regulations, and it targets the **B2B lodge/operator** audience — the high-value buyers who fit the data-and-relationships strategy. This is the article a generalist marketplace will never write.
- **Section outline:**
  1. **Answer-capsule lead:** Yes — if you carry paying passengers you need an **operating licence + a PrDP driver + a roadworthy, correctly-registered conversion**; private own-use is lighter but the conversion must still be legal.
  2. **Two separate questions** — frame explicitly: (A) is the *vehicle* legal, (B) is the *operation* legal.
  3. **(A) Registering a converted game viewer** — homologation via NRCS; "modify" legally = altering seat count; NATIS number; Letter of Authority for modified vehicles. **⚠️ flag the private-conversion path as needing confirmation from NRCS / a specialist.**
  4. **(B) Operating commercially** — NPTR operating licence (NLTA s50), the <3-car courtesy exemption, the **2009+ first-registration cut-off**, Public Passenger Liability Insurance, PrDP for drivers.
  5. **What this means for buyers** — an older (pre-2009) used game viewer **cannot get a new operating licence** for commercial passenger use; fine for private/farm use. This single fact is worth the whole article to a lodge buyer.
  6. **FAQ** (→ FAQPage schema).
  7. **Disclaimer:** "This is a general guide, not legal advice — confirm current requirements with the NRCS, the NPTR/your provincial regulatory entity, and a qualified professional." (Required given the flagged items.)
- **Schema:** FAQPage.
- **Internal links:** `/game-viewers/`, Article 1, `/listings/model/79-series/`.
